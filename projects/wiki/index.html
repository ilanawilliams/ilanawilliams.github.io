What is a dragon?

A dragon is a snake-like legendary creature that appears in folklore of many cultures around the world. Beliefs about dragons vary considerably through regions, but dragons in western cultures since the High Middle Ages have often been depicited as winged, horned, four-liegged and capable of breathing fire. Dragons in eastern culutures are usually depicted as wingless, four-legged, serpentine creatures with above-average intelligence.

Africa

  • An Apep is a giant serpentine creature who resides in the Duat, the Egyptian Underworld. Thunderstorms and earthquake were thought to be caused by Apep's roar.

China

  • The Chinese dragon is the highest-ranking creature in the Chinese animal hierachy.
  • The image of the Chinese dragon was roughly established in the Shang and Zhou dynasties, but there was no great change in a long time.

Ancient Greek and Roman

  • The first mention of a dragon occurs in the Ilaid, in which Agamemnon is described as having a blue dragon motif on his sword belt and an emblem of a three-headed dragon on his breast plate.
  • The Roman Poet, Virgil, described a shepard having to fight a big constricting snake and called it "serpens" and "draco."
